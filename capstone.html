Some Endangered Species



Rusty-Spotted Cat

Also called "hummingbird of the cat family"

It is the smallest wild cat species in the world and is native to India, Sri Lanka and Nepal. It is known its diminutive size, distinctive coat and elusive, noctural nature. The species is listed as near threatened due habitat loss, fragmentation and occasional hunting or conflict with humans.

Red Panda

Also called "lesser panda""red bear-cat" or "fire fox"

The red panda is a small, cat-sized mammal with reddish-brown fur and a ringed tail, native to the Eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Primarily herbivorous, they mainly eat bamboo and are classified as Endangered due to habitat loss. They are solitary, crepuscular, and use an extra "thumb" for climbing and their tail for balance.

Amur Tiger

Also called as(Panthera tigris altaica)the Siberian tiger

Classified as Endangered by the IUCN Red List, these apex predators are found in the Russian Far East and Northeast China. Adapted to cold climates, they have thick fur, pale orange coats, and large paws. Once near extinction in the 1940s with fewer than 50 individuals, conservation efforts have helped their population grow to around 500-600 today.

Blue-throated Macaw

Also called "Glaucous Macaw"

The critically endangered Blue-throated Macaw, native to Bolivia's Beni savannas, has a turquoise-blue head, yellow chest, and blue throat feathers. These long-lived birds nest in palm cavities and feed on fruits, seeds, and nuts. They're threatened by habitat loss, dam flooding, and the illegal pet trade. Conservation efforts focus on protecting their habitat and reducing poaching.
